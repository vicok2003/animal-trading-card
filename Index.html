Gaint panda

The gaint panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca), also known as the panda bear (or simply the panda), is a bear species endemic to china. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.


  • Scientific name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca
  • Height
    • Adult 60-90 cm
    • Babies 6inches long
  • Average lifespan: 20yrs

  • Habitat: Pandas live mainly in temperate forests high in the mountain of southwest china, where they subsist almost entirely on bamboo. They must eat around 26 to 84 pounds of it every day, depending on what part of the bamboo they are eating.


The gaint panda has a body shape typical of bears. It has black fur on its ears, eye patches, limbs and shoulders. The rest of the animal's coat is white. The bear's distinctive coat appears to serve as camouflage in both winter and summer enviroments.

The name "gaint panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda, a neighboring musteloid. Thhough it belongs to the order carnivora, the gaint panda is a folivore, with bamboo shoots and leaves making up more than 99% of its diet. Gaint pandas in the wild occasionally eat other grasses, wild tubers, or even meat in the form of birds, rodents, or carrion. In captivity, they may receive honey, eggs, fish, yams, shrubs leaves, oranges, or bananas along with specially prepared food.
